The 10 most streamed video games

By: Tony Polanco Feb. 8, 2016 3:37 pm

Say what you will about the act of watching others play video games instead of actually playing them yourself, but video game streams have become huge over the past few years and only seem to get bigger each day. Twitch has been at the forefront of this phenomenon, with other services like UStream and even YouTube getting in on the action as well.

Since game streaming is here to stay, we’ve decided that it would be a great idea to track the top streamed games on Twitch and other services each week. We’re able to do this thanks to tools provided by Gamoloco, a stream-monitoring service. These charts are based on the nearly million people and more than 26,000 channels on streaming services worldwide.

Here are the top 10 streamed games from February 1 to 7:

  1. League of Legends (22,703,372 hours watched)
  2. Dota 2 (9,688,853 hours)
  3. Hearthstone: Heroes of Warcraft (8,813,318 hours)
  4. Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (8,437,020 hours)
  5. Call of Duty: Black Ops III (5,262,717 hours)
  6. XCOM2 (2,777,702 hours)
  7. Minecraft (2,238,235 hours)
  8. Gaming Talk Shows* (1,962,004 hours)
  9. H1Z1 (1,766,414 hours)
  10. World of Warcraft (1,636,759 hours)

* The Gaming Talk Shows category is a compilation of all the various gaming talk shows on Twitch.

Here are the top 10 channels and streamers from the same time period:

  1. Riot Games (League of Legends, 4,585,872 hours)
  2. gameshow_cggoen (Counter-Strike: GO, 1,251,983 hours)
  3. imaqtpie (League of Legends, 1,085,830 hours)
  4. lirik (Naruto Shippuden: Ultimate Ninja Storm 4, 940,020 hours)
  5. nightblue3 (League of Legends, 868,867 hours)
  6. nl_kripp (Hearthstone, 803,524 hours)
  7. mushisgosu (League of Legends, 788,700 hours)
  8. athenelive (Hearthstone, 777,097 hours)
  9. trick2g (League of Legends, 771,218 hours)
  10. arteezy (Dota 2, 770,578 hours)

As you can see from the top streamed games, League of Legends utterly crushes the competition. It has more hours watched than the following two games combined, and almost as much as the following three. Many of the games in the top five, specially the first four, are mainstays on streaming services and likely won’t change too often.

I’m actually surprised to see how many of the top streamers are from the United Kingdom. In fact, every single one of them is from the UK. I don’t really know the exact reason why our friends from across the pond seem to be the most popular streamers, but it’s something worth investigating.
